Emerging-Market-Related Fund Flows and Fund Allocations

Daily flows and monthly positions of EM-related funds

"Like the EPFR data, but better"

Trounceflow happened because I grew frustrated with the weekly "EFPR" data when I was as a sell-side analyst.

Trounceflow, like EPFR, makes fund flows and fund allocations data for the same set of mutual funds and ETFs that are either (i) dedicated to emerging market debt and emerging market equity, or (ii) that do not have a dedicated EM mandate but who nonetheless make a meaningful allocation to those asset classes, such as Global Bond funds or Multi-Asset funds.

The Trounceflow difference is:
(1) higher frequency: daily, not weekly, fund flows
(2) shorter lag: one day, not one week, for fund flows
(3) greater detail about how funds are allocating assets
(4) lower cost

Trusted Advisor

Daily insights on what the flows imply for the markets

Short, to-the-point emails

As a former sell-side analyst specialising in flows and positions for Emerging Markets Macro, I know that investment professionals want short, to-the-point insight about what all the flows and positions data actually mean for the markets. Are they telling a good or bad story overall? What about for specific markets?

We email a daily and a weekly overall summary, and about twenty market-specific emails per month (about one per day), telling you exactly this, and no more. In less than one minute per day, you are up to speed with the world's best research from the world's best data.
